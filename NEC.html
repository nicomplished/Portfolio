The National Eagle Center

Nonprofit Website Redesign


THE PROBLEM: Users find it difficult to access important information, such as the scheduling platform, because of the complex layout on The National Eagle Centers’ website. Teachers that are planning a trip for their students should easily be able to find an available time for their trip. How might we be able to improve the location of information needed in order to help users seamlessly book a trip to The National Eagle Center?

MY ROLE: UX Researcher, UX designer, UI Designer

PARTNERS: MaKenna Berger & Grant Douglas

TOOLS: Figma, Google Docs, Miro


Research Stage

MaKenna, Grant, and I began our research by contacting the National Eagle Center via email. We also left a voicemail for them to call us back. Unfortunately, we did not receive a response in time, and though they wanted to help us, our timelines just did not match up.

We also looked at and analyzed other bird and eagle centers within the United States to see how they organized their websites. We looked at The Raptor Center at the University of Minnesota, The American Eagle Foundation, and The Friends of Roberts Bird Sanctuary, Inc.

Heuristic evaluations of the current site took place to determine the most important parts that needed to be changed and we created a proto-persona.

User Testing

Together, we created a user testing plan for the current site, conducted four user tests, and analyzed the results in the form of an affinity diagram to create our user persona, Lily.

Definition

User Insight: Lily needs to be able to seamlessly book a class field trip to The National Eagle Center (28 students and 5 chaperones) for this coming spring. In order to prepare for this field trip, she must also prepare a lesson plan ahead of time.

Problem Statement: Users find it difficult to access important information, such as the scheduling platform, because of the complex layout on The National Eagle Centers’ website. Teachers that are planning a trip for their students should easily be able to find an available time for their trip. How might we be able to improve the location of information needed in order to help users seamlessly book a trip to The National Eagle Center?

After defining our project, we came up with a UX scenario of Lily scheduling a group trip to the NEC, a storyboard, and created a new sitemap by sorting cards.

Prototyping

Each of us created paper prototypes for the new home page. After analyzing each sketch, we moved on to a low-fidelity digital prototype for each page and created a styleguide. We preformed user testing on our low-fi prototype and recorded our testers' notes.

From there, we moved on to the mid-fi and hi-fi prototypes, as well as the mobile version.

Division of Labor

Nicole (Me): Competitive Analysis, Proto Persona, User Persona, Storyboard, Card Sorting, Sitemap, Mid-Fidelity Prototype, High-Fidelity Prototype, Mobile Prototype

MaKenna: Stakeholder Contact, User Insight, Problem Statement, Heuristic Evaluation, Sitemap, Mid-Fidelity Prototype, UI Style Guide

Grant: User Testing Plan, Affinity Diagram, I Like, I Wish, What If Map, UX Scenario

Together: User Testing, Feature Prioritization, Paper Prototypes, Presentation

What's Next?

If we choose to move forward with this redesign, we would want to finish the other pages and then contact The National Eagle Center for approval before moving forward. Another element we would like to add to the site would be interactive lesson plans for students and teachers participating in distance learning.
